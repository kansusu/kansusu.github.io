I am a Ph.D. candidate at Brandeis University, working with Professor James Pustejovsky in the Lab for Linguistics and Computation. I study how people and AI agents build, track, and repair common ground in collaborative dialogue — drawing on Theory of Mind, multimodal perception, and LLM alignment. I am interested in agents that don't just answer well but maintain shared belief states with their human collaborators, especially under partial, asymmetric information.

Before Brandeis I completed an M.S. in Computational Linguistics at Georgetown, an M.A. in Linguistics at UCL, and a B.A. in Philosophy and Chinese Literature at Xiamen University. See my research page for current directions and publications for a full list.
